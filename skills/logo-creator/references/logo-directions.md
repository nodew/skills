# Logo Directions

Use this reference when proposing directions before creating the final logo.

## What Makes Directions Useful

Directions should differ in structure, not just adjectives. A good set of directions gives the user meaningful choices without exploding the scope.

## Recommended Direction Types

### Symbol-led

Best for:

- apps needing a strong icon
- products that need social avatar recognition
- compact UI placements

Typical structure:

- geometric or conceptual mark
- optional compact wordmark pairing

Risk:

- abstract marks can become generic if not grounded in the product story

### Wordmark-led

Best for:

- longer-term brand recognition
- products with distinctive names
- companies that need clarity more than symbolism

Typical structure:

- custom lettering or subtle letter modification
- simpler supporting icon

Risk:

- may scale poorly at favicon sizes unless paired with a simplified mark

### Monogram or letterform-led

Best for:

- short brand names
- premium or technical brands
- app icons where initials matter

Typical structure:

- initials or a single letter transformed into a symbol

Risk:

- initials can feel interchangeable if the idea is not specific enough

### Combination or emblem-led

Best for:

- brands that need both a memorable icon and clear name recognition
- products with narrative or thematic identity (badges, crests, contained marks)
- contexts where the logo must work as a single unit rather than separable parts

Typical structure:

- text integrated into or enclosed by a shape or illustration
- the symbol and text are designed as one inseparable lockup

Risk:

- can lose legibility at small sizes if the emblem contains too much detail
- harder to separate into independent mark and wordmark variants

## Direction Template

Use concise structure:

```text
Direction 1: Symbol-led precision
- Concept: A modular mark built from intersecting paths that suggests routing and clarity.
- Why it fits: Works well for infrastructure or workflow products that need a strong icon.
- Tradeoff: Can feel cold if the typography is not softened.
```

## Recommendation Rule

Recommend one direction by default. Explain why it best balances:

- distinctiveness
- small-size legibility
- fit to audience
- usefulness across app icon, web, and document contexts
